# social

Posts and short clips that carry a launch and keep an indie/OSS project visible afterward. Scope it to where this project's audience actually is (from `GROWTH.md` → Channels); ignore the full SaaS social-calendar machine.

## Platforms that fit

| Platform | Why | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| **X / Twitter** | Indie + dev community, build-in-public culture. | A few posts around each release; occasional build-in-public. |
| **Mastodon (fosstodon, etc.)** | Strong open-source / dev audience; rewards FOSS. | Same as X; a natural home for an OSS tool. |
| **Reddit** | Covered in `launch` for posts; here for ongoing genuine participation in the project's niche subs. | Be a member, not a broadcaster. |
| **Short video (Shorts/Reels)** | A 15–30s demo clip can travel for a visual tool. | Optional, per release. |

Skip LinkedIn carousels, TikTok trend-chasing, and daily-posting calendars. They don't fit a free maker tool and burn time for little return.

## What to post

- **The demo asset.** The tool working is the post. Lead with it.
- **Build-in-public notes.** A real progress update with a screenshot. Developers follow real progress.
- **Technical deep-dives.** A thread on the hard/interesting part of the project (the algorithm, the architecture, the constraint you solved). These get reshared and indexed.
- **Honest comparisons.** "Why I built an open alternative to [incumbent]" — credit the incumbent, state what's different (from `GROWTH.md` / `position`).
- **Release announcements.** Link the release, show the headline change.

## Hooks (first line decides everything)

Build each from a real differentiator in `GROWTH.md`. Patterns:

- "[What it does], free and open source: [link]"
- "I wanted [incumbent] but [the gap], so I built it."
- "[Project] now [new capability]."
- "How I [solved the hard technical thing]:"

Keep them true. No "revolutionary," no fake urgency, no fabricated milestones.

## Short-form video

If you make a clip:

- 9:16 for Shorts/Reels, 16:9 or 1:1 for X.
- First second: visual hook (the tool doing its thing) + a one-line text overlay.
- 15–30s total; show the differentiating features; end with the repo URL.
- Add captions; most social video is watched muted.
- Authentic screen-recording beats over-production. No AI avatars needed for a dev tool.

## Engagement

- Reply to everyone, including critics. "Good point, tracked in issue #N" builds more trust than defensiveness.
- When someone shares or reviews the project, thank them and (with permission) quote real feedback later.
- Comment genuinely on adjacent projects. Reciprocity in maker niches is real and small.

## Deliverable

For a given moment (launch, release, feature): the platform list (from `GROWTH.md`), 2–3 post drafts with hooks (each pointing at a concrete asset and the repo), and the clip spec if video is involved. Reuse the asset from `visuals` rather than inventing new ones.
